About Me

I’m Edward Laurence C. Estrella, a first-year BS Computer Science student at Pamantasan ng Lungsod ng Maynila. I am from Marilao, Bulacan. My interest in technology started in high school and grew stronger through various projects. These experiences shaped my decision to pursue computer science and continue exploring innovation.

Outside of school, I spend my time on hobbies that keep me creative and active. Gaming inspires my curiosity and love for stories. Cycling gives me a way to relax and stay healthy. I also enjoy working with tech and experimenting with computers. These interests push me to keep learning, growing, and challenging myself in both academics and personal life.

Hobbies and Interests

Gaming

I’ve always been surrounded by games growing up. I started with simple browser games like Farmville, then moved on to Minecraft and Roblox. I also explored MMOs like Club Penguin and even emulated old Pokémon games. During the pandemic, I discovered shooter games such as Call of Duty Mobile and Apex Legends, which introduced me to the thrill of fast-paced, competitive gameplay. Over time, I found myself drawn to story-rich RPGs and JRPGs, with the Persona series becoming a major influence on me. I’ve played Persona 2 through Persona 5, and the series really shaped my love for immersive storytelling and character-driven experiences. Later on, a friend introduced me to gacha games, starting with Genshin Impact, and since then I’ve tried more than a dozen of them. I’m still playing Wuthering Waves and Persona 5: The Phantom X today, alongside other titles like Cyberpunk 2077 and Project Zomboid.

Cycling

When I was little, my dad got into cycling and made lots of friends who would ride with him to different places. Occasionally, I would join their rides. One memory that stood out to me was our cycling tour around Corregidor Island, where our group explored the historic remains of the eerie island on our bikes. Cycling became a special bonding activity with my dad. Due to life, he quit after a couple of years and so did I. During the pandemic however, I picked it back up again as an escape from being stuck at home. My dad would occasionally join me too. I found cycling to be very relaxing and a great way to relieve stress. Over time, I even made friends I could ride with. Unfortunately, due to college and the demands of life, cycling has become less frequent for me, though it still holds a special place in me.

Tech

My interest in video games naturally led me to develop a strong curiosity for technology. To play the latest and greatest games, I had to learn about the right specifications and components. I also started keeping track of the newest products and technologies being released. During the pandemic, I found some old PCs and laptops that were broken. I tinkered with them and managed to get a few working again. In Grade 10, our computer subject focused on IT topics. We learned how to disassemble and reassemble a PC, identify its different parts, and crimp network cables. Those experiences made me appreciate both gaming and the technology that makes it possible.
